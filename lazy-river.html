🌊 NOW ENTERING: THE ROUTE 🌊
2. The Route & Layout Breakdown

You launch from the main tube dock near the cable park boathouse, drifting out under the shadow of the wakeboard cable towers as riders carve overhead. The current is lazy here on purpose — this opening stretch is all about letting your eyes adjust and your tube find its spin. Around the quarter-mile mark you pass beneath the first shaded bridge crossing, a wooden trestle that doubles as a photo spot for people walking the perimeter path above you.

The river then bends past the wave pool overlook, where you can watch the reef break doing its thing before ducking into the "surge channel" — a narrower, faster-moving stretch where hidden jets kick the current up to give you a little push, MEGAVISION's favorite 40 seconds of the entire loop. From there it's a long, wide, scenery stretch past the surf lagoon fencing and a second, shorter tunnel crossing (concrete, cooler than the rest of the river by a good five degrees, everyone agrees on this). The final third loops back past the original cable park lakefront, gently depositing you back at the dock right where you started, 3,000 feet later.

3. Park & Logistics Context

🏃 PLANNING YOUR VISIT
Entry RestrictionsRiders under 48in must wear a life vest and be accompanied by an adult on the river at all times
Peak Crowd HoursWeekend afternoons (1–5pm) and Texas summer holiday weeks — go early or go late
Best Tube PickupThe main dock stand by the boathouse rarely runs dry; the far-side stand near the surge channel almost always does
Nearest FoodDockside snack stall, roughly 40 yards from the launch point
Nearest RestroomsChanging/restroom facility just past the wave pool overlook, about two-thirds of the way around the loop

4. Historical Timeline

📅 TIMELINE
Pre-2016Operating for years as BSR Cable Park, a wakeboard cable park on the same Waco lakefront
2016–2019Major expansion into BSR Surf Resort, adding the reef wave pool and surrounding waterpark infrastructure
July 2020The 3,000 ft lazy river is certified by Guinness World Records as the Longest Lazy River ever built
Since 2020Remains one of the marquee attractions of BSR Surf Resort, still floated daily by tourists and Texans alike
